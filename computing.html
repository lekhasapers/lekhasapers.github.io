computing

Largely, I am a self-taught programmer, working with Python, SQL, and LAMP. Much of my formalized work explores computational humanities—bridging media, research, and journalism, though I often extend beyond these parameters. This website, for instance, was hand-coded with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

By nature, I am split by two equal parts: technologist, and humanist. I learned to code by writing stories with Python, a testament to my unique composition.

This is further demonstrated by one of the highlights of my programming career.

Working at 91.9 KCSB FM in Santa Barbara, I developed a fully operational database for KCSB's archival collection. I digitized and uploaded over 50 archives, catalogued over three thousand entries, and preserved a substantial portion of KCSB's 60-year archival history. This database not only highlights my technical abilities but also provides the KCSB community with a structured, accessible way to celebrate and preserve their history—organized according to Dublin Core metadata standards.
